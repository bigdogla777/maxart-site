The Artist

Meet Maxine

With over six decades in fine arts and textile design, I create pieces rooted in tradition but shaped by contemporary life. My paintings explore color relationships and texture, while my purses are functional art crafted for everyday elegance. And, you need to wear your daughter's heirloom. — Maxine

About the Artist

Maxine's desire for creative expression seemed to exist before she picked up her first drawing instrument. In early childhood her interest in art turned to painting. She trained at the Washington School of Art in Washington, D.C., Loveland Art Academy in Colorado, and Scottsdale Art Academy, and studied with David Leffel, Charles Cross, Terry Issac, Rod Frederick, Margaret Kessler, Robert Bateman, and Quang Ho.

Her earliest success came in Florida in the late 1970s with paintings of landscapes, seascapes, and birds. There she was commissioned to paint four Florida landscapes by the City of Vero Beach. Professionals throughout Florida purchased her early works — including the Mayor and City Attorney of Kissimmee, residents of Johns Island, and the Senior Vice President of Yamaha Motor Corporation. Her paintings are enjoyed by art lovers from the East to the West Coasts.

In the early 1990s she moved to Colorado, and her art evolved to the still lifes, landscapes, and animals that are her signature today. Still life painting — her favorite — follows the style of the Old Masters with modern and timeless objects. Her interest in wine has transferred to her art in her newest series, “Wine & Companions.” Her medium is oil on panel.

Her recognition spans a long career: a third-place finish in the Ducks Unlimited Stamp Contest, the Winsor & Newton Award at the Heritage Fine Art Show, the People's Choice Award at the Thompson Art Show, First Place in the Scottsdale Artist School Show, and acceptance into the National Oil & Acrylic Painters' Society Show. Festival appearances include La Quinta (CA), Boulder, Evergreen, Breckenridge, and Beaver Creek (CO), Bayou City and Cottonwood (TX), plus Impressions Gallery and the Turner Art Gallery in Denver.

Maxine's paintings are noted for not merely recording a scene but for setting a mood. Her great sense of color, combined with her sense of design, creates the harmony of shapes and light that enchants viewers.

Limited Reproductions

Remarqued, Artist-Proof Giclée Fine Art Reproductions

Giclée (pronounced “zhee-clay”) reproductions have the look and feel of an original piece of art, and will look good for a hundred years when properly displayed. Prints on canvas can be up to 52 inches wide and unlimited in length. Pricing is according to size, unframed and unmounted — write for specific quotes.
