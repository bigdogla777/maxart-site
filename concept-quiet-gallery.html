About the Artist

Maxine Orndorff Gregory

“With over six decades in fine arts and textile design, I create pieces rooted in tradition but shaped by contemporary life. My paintings explore color relationships and texture, while my purses are functional art crafted for everyday elegance.” — Maxine

Maxine’s desire for creative expression seemed to exist before she picked up her first drawing instrument. In early childhood her interest turned to painting. She trained at the Washington School of Art in Washington, D.C., the Loveland Art Academy in Colorado, and the Scottsdale Art Academy, and studied with David Leffel, Charles Cross, Terry Issac, Rod Frederick, Margaret Kessler, Robert Bateman, and Quang Ho.

Her earliest success came in Florida in the late 1970s with paintings of landscapes, seascapes, and birds, including four Florida landscapes commissioned by the City of Vero Beach. Her paintings are enjoyed by art lovers from the East to the West Coast.

In the early 1990s she moved to Colorado, where her art evolved to the still lifes, landscapes, and animals that are her signature today. Still life painting, her favorite, follows the style of the Old Masters with modern and timeless objects; her interest in wine carries into her newest series, Wine & Companions. Her medium is oil on panel.

Her awards range from ribbons in regional shows to prizes in major competitions: a third-place finish in the Ducks Unlimited Stamp Contest, the Winsor & Newton Award at the Heritage Fine Art Show, the People’s Choice Award at the Thompson Art Show, First Place in the Scottsdale Artist School Show, and acceptance into the National Oil & Acrylic Painters’ Society Show. Festival appearances from La Quinta and Boulder to Breckenridge, Beaver Creek, and the Bayou City Art Festival — along with Impressions Gallery and the Turner Art Gallery in Denver — have steadily grown demand among collectors.

Maxine’s paintings are noted not merely for recording a scene but for setting a mood: a great sense of color, combined with her sense of design, creates the harmony of shapes and light that enchants viewers.
