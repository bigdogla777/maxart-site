The Story · Meet Maxine

A life spent making beautiful things

Maxine's desire for creative expression seemed to exist before she picked up her first drawing instrument. In early childhood her interest in art turned to painting. She trained at the Washington School of Art in Washington, D.C., the Loveland Art Academy in Colorado, and the Scottsdale Art Academy, and studied with David Leffel, Charles Cross, Terry Issac, Rod Frederick, Margaret Kessler, Robert Bateman, and Quang Ho.

Her earliest success came in Florida in the late 1970s with paintings of landscapes, seascapes, and birds. She was commissioned to paint four Florida landscapes by the City of Vero Beach, and professionals throughout the state — including the Mayor and City Attorney of Kissimmee — purchased her early works for their offices and homes. Her paintings are enjoyed by art lovers from the East Coast to the West.

In the early 1990s she moved to Colorado, and her art evolved to the still lifes, landscapes, and animals that are her signature today. Still life painting, her favorite, follows the style of the Old Masters with modern and timeless objects; her love of wine flows into her newest series, Wine & Companions. Her medium is oil on panel.

Her awards run the gamut from regional ribbons to major competitions: a third-place finish in the Ducks Unlimited Stamp Contest, the Winsor & Newton Award at the Heritage Fine Art Show, the People's Choice Award at the Thompson Art Show, First Place at the Scottsdale Artist School Show, and acceptance into the National Oil & Acrylic Painters' Society Show. From the La Quinta Art Festival to Beaver Creek and the Turner Art Gallery in Denver, demand among collectors keeps growing.

With over six decades in fine arts and textile design, Maxine creates pieces rooted in tradition but shaped by contemporary life — paintings that explore color and texture, and purses that are functional art crafted for everyday elegance.

The Paintings

Oil on panel, in the manner of the Old Masters

Maxine's paintings are noted not for merely recording a scene, but for setting a mood. Her sense of color, joined with her sense of design, creates the harmony of shape and light that enchants viewers — still lifes, Colorado landscapes, wildlife, and the wine that keeps them company.
